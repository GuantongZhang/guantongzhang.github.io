Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Jie & Maggie Real Estate Team collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you visit this website or contact us about real estate services in California.

1. Scope

This policy applies to information collected through this website, contact forms, phone calls, email, and related online interactions. It does not apply to third-party websites or services linked from this site.

2. What Data We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Identifiers and contact details (name, phone number, email address).
  • Client communications (messages, inquiry details, requested services).
  • Real estate preference information (budget range, location interests, property criteria).
  • Transaction-related information you provide during brokerage services.
  • Internet or network activity (IP address, browser type, device information, pages viewed, approximate geolocation).

3. How We Use Personal Information

  • To respond to inquiries and provide requested services.
  • To communicate about listings, showings, offers, escrows, and related transactions.
  • To operate, secure, and improve this website and service quality.
  • To comply with legal, regulatory, tax, accounting, and recordkeeping obligations.

4. Whether We Sell or Share Data

We do not sell personal information for money. We do not knowingly share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

If our practices change in the future, we will update this policy and provide any legally required notice and opt-out mechanism.

5. Who We Disclose Data To

We may disclose personal information to service providers and professional partners as needed to operate our business, including website hosting, form processing, transaction coordination, legal/accounting support, and compliance support. We may also disclose information when required by law.

6. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, and where applicable under the California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended by the CPRA), you may have the right to:

  • Know/access categories and specific pieces of personal information collected, used, and disclosed.
  • Request deletion of personal information, subject to legal exceptions.
  • Request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Opt out of sale/share of personal information (if applicable).
  • Limit use/disclosure of sensitive personal information (if applicable).
  • Receive non-discriminatory treatment for exercising privacy rights.
